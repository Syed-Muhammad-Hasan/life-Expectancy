Life Expectancy Data Analysis

Life expectancy tells us how long people usually live in a certain place. It's a big deal because it shows how healthy and happy a group of people generally are. This can be because of good healthcare, having enough money, and living in a nice place. In this project, we're going to look at lots of different things that might make people live longer or shorter lives. We'll look at how this has changed over time and compare it in different parts of the world and for people with different amounts of money. By doing this, we hope to find out why some people live longer than others.

Life Expectancy at a glance!

The choropleth map provides a visual representation of global life expectancy trends from 2000 to 2015, highlighting significant regional disparities and overall progress. High life expectancy, often exceeding 80 years, is predominantly seen in North America, Western Europe, and parts of East Asia, indicating the benefits of advanced healthcare systems and higher living standards. In contrast, regions like Sub-Saharan Africa and parts of South Asia exhibit lower life expectancy, often below 60 years, due to factors like poverty, limited healthcare access, and higher disease prevalence. The map's interactive features, such as zooming and hover effects, enhance the exploration of these patterns, showing both the strides made globally and the areas needing further improvement.

Regional Life Expectancy

The line chart depicts the life expectancy trends across different regions from 2000 to 2015, offering insights into how life expectancy varies globally. Each line represents a region, with color-coded legends facilitating easy identification. Hovering over a line or legend highlights the corresponding data, while a tooltip provides detailed information about life expectancy and date upon mouseover on individual data points. The chart's axes are labeled for clarity, with the x-axis denoting years and the y-axis representing life expectancy. This visualization aids in understanding how life expectancy has evolved over time across various regions

Developed VS Developing

The scatter plot illustrates the relationship between GDP and life expectancy for various countries, categorized by their development status, from 2000 to 2015. Higher GDP generally correlates with longer life expectancy, highlighting the role of economic prosperity in enhancing public health and longevity. Developed countries, depicted in blue, consistently show higher life expectancy at a given GDP level compared to developing countries, represented in red. This discrepancy underscores the multifaceted nature of development, where factors such as healthcare infrastructure, education, and social services also significantly influence health outcomes. The interactive elements, including tooltips and color-coded statuses, provide a nuanced understanding of how economic and developmental contexts impact life expectancy across the globe.

Top and Bottom

The bar graphs for the top 10 and bottom 10 countries by life expectancy provide a detailed comparison of the best and worst-performing nations in terms of health outcomes for each selected year from 2000 to 2015. The charts highlight significant disparities between the highest and lowest life expectancies, reflecting varying levels of healthcare, economic development, and social conditions across countries. Developed countries consistently populate the top 10, showcasing higher life expectancies, while developing countries often appear in the bottom 10, indicating the challenges they face in improving health outcomes. The color-coded bars based on development status and interactive tooltips enhance the visualization's clarity, making it easier to discern and understand the data's implications.

Statistical Analysis

The box plot visualization provides an insightful comparison of life expectancy distributions between developed and developing countries from 2000 to 2015. By selecting a year, users can see how the life expectancies differ significantly across these two groups. The box plot highlights key statistical measures such as the median, quartiles, and ranges, clearly demonstrating the higher life expectancy in developed countries compared to developing ones.

Life Expectancy Over Time

The density plots dynamically illustrate the evolution of life expectancy trends over the years 2000 to 2015, providing insights into the distribution of life expectancy across different countries.
