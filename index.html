Life Expectancy Data Analysis

In this project, we will be using life expextancy dataset, available here. The dataset consists of life expectancy and health factors for 193 countries collected by the WHO. We will be analysing this dataset and make useful results by which you can decide how long will you live.

Life Expectancy at a glance!

Here we are showing life expectancies of different countries at the very top level by using chloropeth maps. We have cleaned the dataset by dropping the countries for which life expectancy data was not available. Furthermore, from the dataset the data for occupied/conflicted states are not available.

Developed VS Developing

It is important to do life expectancy analysis based on country status i.e. either it will be "Developed" or "Developing". Along with that status an important factor to analyse is countries GDP. This combination gives a comprehensive overview over the relationship between economic performance and health outcomes. Moreover, we also have sorted top 10 and bottom 10 countries by life expectancies. The color of each bar represents the status of country. These two charts provide powerful insights into the global health landscape. They highlight the disparities between developing and developed nations.

Box Plot of Life Expectancy

In our journey to understand global life expectancy, we have examined various facets such as the geographical distribution and economic factors. Now, let's dive deeper into the distribution of life expectancy across developed and developing countries using box plots. This type of visualization is crucial because it not only shows the median life expectancy but also illustrates the range and variability within each category. By visualizing life expectancy through box plots, we can gain insights into the spread of life expectancy values, identify outliers, and compare the central tendencies of developed and developing countries. This will help us understand the disparities in health outcomes between these two groups and highlight areas needing attention. In the previous sections, we observed how GDP and country status influence life expectancy through scatter plots and bar graphs. The box plots will complement these visualizations by providing a clear picture of the distribution and variability of life expectancy within each group, adding another layer to our analysis.

Bubble Chart

Bubble Chart to visualize relations between life expectancy and different factors. The bigger the bubble, the bigger the population. 2015 data have many missing values (NaN), that's why filtering on this year somo graphs have very few bubbles.

Density plot

Density plot of global life expectancy and the probability of death using a Kernel Density Estimator (KDE). The play button helps to visualize the changes over the years. Each tick on the animation after clicking it represents a year (from 2000 since the beginning to end on 2015).
